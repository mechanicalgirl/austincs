Welcome to the Austin CSTA Project

Welcome! This project came about from a desire to build relationships between members of the local programming community and computer science educators through the local chapter of the Computer Science Teachers Association here in Austin.

What we're trying to do: get developers into the classrooms, to have them talk to students about their careers and maybe get involved directly, through mentoring and teaching.

How it works: programming professionals and computer science educators can sign up, get paired and make plans for a school visit. These classroom visits can include:

  • a Q&A with students about what software development careers are like and what kinds of things they needed to learn
  • live demonstrations of coding work
  • one-on-one mentoring, or extras such as helping to plan an after-school computer club

All of that is up to the teacher and developer, who are free to negotiate whatever they'd like to see presented to students.

What you'll find on this site: Separate signups for teachers and developers, plus (coming) a page of presentation ideas and activity resources that developers can use when planning their visits.

Thanks for taking an interest in computer science classrooms! I'm looking forward to seeing what an impact this program makes on Austin area students this year!
